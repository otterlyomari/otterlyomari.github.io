---
title: 'No more am I the same'
description: 'A solemn poem exploring transformation, identity loss, and quiet hope in the aftermath of social death, reflecting on what remains when connection and self-perception are stripped away.'
type: 'Poetry'
tags: ['poem', 'melancholy', 'angst']
pubDate: 2025-04-05
---

The echoes call from days gone by,
whispers carved in stone and sky.
Once I stumbled, once I fell,
left behind a trail to hell.

The hands I’ve hurt, the hearts I’ve torn,
the silent cries, the love I scorned—
they haunt me still, they shape my name,
but I will not be born in shame.

No path repeats, no step rewinds,
the past can scar, but not define.
I’ve bled, I’ve learned, I’ve faced the cost,
and vowed to never more be lost.

No careless words, no reckless hands,
no hollow trust on shifting sands.
I walk with purpose, slow and true,
for I am not the man they knew.

Let time judge me, let wounds mend,
let trust be something I defend.
I swear by all that lies ahead—
I will not break, I will not bend.
