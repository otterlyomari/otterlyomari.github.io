My heart tore to shreds,
Frayed at the edges, thread by thread.
Once, I walked beside the fierce and free,
Now I chase the ghost of what used to be.

Beneath the moon, your eyes would burn,
A crimson flame, where shadows turn.
Your fur like midnight’s deepest shade,
A soul that glowed, a love that swayed.

You moved like lightning through the dark,
A spark of strength, a glowing mark.
But I, too reckless, broke the line,
Wove words that fractured what was mine.

In that moment, the world turned cold,
My trust in pieces, scattered, sold.
Now I stand alone in the fray,
A wolf once close, now far away.

I long to stitch the wounds I made,
To gather all the broken shade,
But fear you’ve shredded all that’s left,
And left me with the bitter theft.

The bond we shared, now turned to dust,
My deeds unworthy of your trust.
I ache, I burn, I shatter still,
But you, no longer here, are still.

What remains? A name, a whispered call,
A shadowed memory, I can’t recall—
Did I mean enough for you to stay?
Or did I, in my folly, chase you away?

Shreds of my hope fall like ash,
Scattered by the winds of the past.
Could I have fixed it? Should I have tried?
Or is this distance where I’ve died?

I’ll carry you through every night,
A fractured bond that fades from sight.
The hero, lost in shifting shreds,
A heart that beats, but cannot mend.
