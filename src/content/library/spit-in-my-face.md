Spit in my face— I've learned not to flinch,
Your words cut deep, but I won’t give an inch.
Call me insane, call me lost, call me weak,
But you’re the one who taught me not to speak.

I stumble away, but I’ll stumble back,
Drawn to the fire, to the love that we lacked.
Smoke fills the air in that attic of lies,
While you laugh at the wreckage behind vacant eyes.

Wasted, tasting the venom you poured,
Knowing too well that you poisoned the chord.
I bled for you, carved love in my skin,
Yet you watched with a smile, never letting me in.

Crushed beneath whispers, shattered by touch,
You swore you adored me—was that asking too much?
Now here I stand, with nothing but pain,
Bleeding for love that won’t come back again.
