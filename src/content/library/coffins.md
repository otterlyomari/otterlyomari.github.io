Beneath the soil, where silence grows,
A box once held what no one knows—
The tattered dreams, the ghost of flame,
The name I wore, no longer same.

I died in pieces, slow and true,
Each splintered truth, a shade of blue.
But darkness doesn't only grieve—
It seeds the things we can’t believe.

The coffin cracked with roots of light,
A bloom began beneath the night.
Petals rose from splintered pain,
Drinking deep the fall of rain.

Ash became the richest ground,
Where broken hearts made sacred sound.
Not an end, but something new—
A self reborn in morning dew.

So bury me when I must fall,
I promise I will rise from all.
From every death, a softer room—
The soul reblooms in coffins bloom.
